Mysterious Myanmar

responsive website

Overview

A land of breathtaking beauty and fascinating cultural traditions for many years was inaccessible to tourists. Now they have an opportunity to visit beautiful and mysterious Myanmar. I created the landing page to pay attention to those who are looking for something different for adventure, for relaxation, and for discovery. Myanmar is the most appropriate place for this.

Designing the Brand

Since 1940 the Burmese subspecies of the Java peacock is one of the national symbols of Myanmar (the second national bird in the world; the first is the bald eagle, the national symbol of the United States). I made a peacock’s silhouette as part of the logo. The final design was selected based on the preference test results.

User Survey

I started user research through a survey, as quick and useful method for gathering customer’s feedback. 70% of 20 respondents travel 1-2 times a year. 80% of respondents travel couple or with family. With different likelihood 70% of respondents would like to visit Myanmar and stay there for 1-1.5 weeks. Respondents often use travel blogs (40%) and travel websites (30%) to pick the destination. 60% of respondents plan their trip themselves and 40% of them use the services of travel agencies.

Wireframes & Testing

The initial idea was to focus on the country and make a non-traditional landing page with lots of photos of Myanmar sights that are recommended for visiting. All photos were placed in a mosaic collage. This structure enabled to create a responsive layout. Clicking on the CTA-button a user transfered to the travel agency’s website where they could find full information about trip, available dates, hotels, and etc.

I created a wireframe for a test on UsabilityHub.com. I got feedback from 50 respondents. Most of them liked the design, layout and photos, however almost half of the participants noticed that there's too much information there, and overall the mockup was perceived as the travel agency website. It was not the goal of the landing page creation.

Mockups

I created the other landing page design in a more traditional style focused on information about travel agency services.

Design A

Design B

I made the changes below:

  • Reduced the amount of textual information about the country and added Myanmar map
  • Added information about travel agency services
  • Replaced the bulk of photos on a few photos of must-see places
  • Added the agency staff’s photos
  • Added the video of one of the Myanmar travelers

Testing 2

I conducted a preference test of both landing page designs. Based on Usability Hub, Design B worked better with a success rate of 60% as compare to Design A with a success rate of 40%. The 20% gap between the two alternatives was not statistically significant. However, the individuals that selected Design A had taken a little more time choosing it as their preference compared to Design B. The test also had a small sample size, which may not reflect an accurate representation of users’ preferences. Based on the results of all the tests, I created a final mockup.

Coding

I used HTML5/CSS3 (Flexbox layout) to bring the landing page to life. In order to ensure users could access the product on different devices, the experience was coded to be responsive.

view responsive demo

Learning

The project was a good learning experience, I especially loved testing designs on users. This tight feedback loop helped take ambiguity out of my designs, and it felt good to produce designs with the confidence that users would enjoy and understand. Do not be afraid to give up or change various parts of your ideas!
