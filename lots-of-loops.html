Lots of Loops

online boutique

Problem

Online stores exist for everything. Besides, there is Etsy – a giant online marketplace where hand-makers offer their products without spending a lot of time and effort on creating their own online shop. Create an online boutique that offers unique handmade items – knitted and crocheted toys, accessories, and knitwear.

 

Solution

The best way to design an elegant shopping experience is to take everything a user would expect from a best-in-class e-commerce site – an intuitive search and navigation, a responsive design on any platform, intelligent merchandising and easy checkout.

Designing the Brand

Knitted fabric consists of a lot of consecutive rows of interlocking loops. So the idea of the logo was that its symbols had to be tied like loops of yarn. Since the boutique specializes in offering high-quality products, I decided that the design style needed to be exquisite, strict, but not boring. Keeping this in mind, I chose the color palette and typography, Josefin font family which is geometric, elegant and kind of vintage, especially for titling. The maroon color with its shades was a primary color and the light yellow was an action color to add a little joy. The final logo design was selected based on the preference test results.

Discovery

In order to understand where Lots of Loops ranked in the market, I took a closer look at some of its competitors. Granted, The Mint Julep Boutique, Modern Vintage Boutique, Extravagantza provide a similar service as Lots of Loops, with strengths in oﬀering easy mobile device browsing or purchase. Social media outreach that encourages users to tag and post photos is also be very essential.

User Survey & Personas

A critical component of the analysis is identifying target audience. From the very start, it was clear that Lots of Loops would have a mainly female audience. 92% of them are mothers or business women. However, the survey revealed that the product could be interesting for active working men also. 89% of respondents buy knitwear, including handmade. 65% of respondents consider that knitwear is not only comfortable and cozy, but also accentuates the personality. Only 2% of respondents want to buy expensive knitted toys for their children, 46% of respondents would be delighted to have it as a gift, and another 39% of respondents want to have a handmade toy as a part of interior or collection. Creating personas helped me fine-tune the design and content that spoke directly to the target audience.

User Flows

I created user flows to map how users may interact with the website and figure out their purchase journey.

Wireframes

I started with a mobile first design wireframes using Sketch3. It was essential to make sure that the web design would be translated correctly on mobile devices. From there, the Home page, the Category page, the Product page, and eventual flows for purchasing and reviewing were created.

Mockups – Show items in the best light

A hero image is like a shop window. I created a collage of the boutique’s products in Photoshop. Here are the source photos.

To add a friendly appearance,  the customers’ photos and the toys’ photos in a live environment were selected for displaying the items on the website. Product’s photography consists of multiple views. This helps to inform a customer as they browse and showcases each product at its best. The Product page includes a full product’s description, size chart, rating, and social media buttons to share, reviews help a customer learn more about the item. In addition, a customer has an opportunity to order a gift box if they buy an item as a gift, or to save this item to their Wish List.

In depth – the Checkout

The checkout process is easy and comfortable when a customer:

  • can change an item, its quantity, size, or delete it from the shopping cart
  • can make purchases without creating an account
  • always knows which stage of a checkout process they are in, so they can easily come back at any time
  • can choose the type of payment
  • can edit all information entered on a review stage
  • can print their order

Prototyping & Testing

I designed mockups using Sketch3 in order to create a clickable prototype using InVision App and send it to Usabilityhub.com for testing. The results showed a clear understanding of Lots of Loops’ purpose – users were able to navigate through different features of the product with ease. Most importantly at this point, when a customer was ready to purchase their item, they could breeze through a checkout experience that is clearly labelled and free from potential distractions.

view prototype

Coding

I used HTML5 and CSS3 (Flexbox layout) to develop the mockups into a responsive site and submit pages for integration into product launch. This portion of the project has helped me work on my front-end development skills and ultimately taught me to become a stronger designer with an understanding of how to bring my designs to life. The product currently has two working pages – the Home page and the Category page.

view responsive demo

Results

This UX/UI experience including my skills as a graphic designer and coding had ultimately helped me become more confident as a well-rounded designer. HTML5/CSS3 skills help me grow as a designer and always consider how different layouts created as wireframes can be developed in code.
