Good Market

grocery shopping app

Problem

Good Market is a medium scale grocery franchise. Although their customer satisfaction ratings have remained relatively constant over the last 4 years, their market share has decreasing by 8% each year. If they continue at this rate for another year or two, they’ll be forced to shut their doors. Through some market and user research they’ve determined that this decline is most likely due to the increase in online grocery ordering & delivery products.

 

Solution

To create a website that allows Good Market’s customers to buy products online, have them delivered promptly, and thus expand their customer base and market share.

Discovery

First, I identified business and user goals, and determined how they could be aligned. I crafted the user goals based on a persona called "The Busy Professional": tech savvy, 25-35-year-old urbanites with disposable income. They work hard and don't have time to grocery shopping. Instead, they love to use their mobile devices to make purchases.

Competitive Analysis

For competitive analysis, I chose three competitors—Google Shopping Express, ShopFoodEx and Thrive Market. This analysis helped me to learn what solutions existed, what worked well and what could be improved.

 

Usability Competitive Analysis

To dive deeper into the competitors’ products I conducted a heuristic review of Google Shopping Express to see how well they solve problems.

 

 

Defining Content & Information Architecture

Card sorting allowed me to see how the potential users naturally categorized the items on the Good Market site, so that I made a more informed decision about how the information is organized. All three participants had different ways of categorizing food types.

I created a sitemap to organize the pages, information, and navigation of the app.

User Flows

Keeping in mind the sitemap and exploring of competitors’ apps, I created user flows for the key tasks within the app: search for products, creating an account, and checkout.

Wireframes

Guided by Information Architecture principles I created wireframes of the product’s key pages.

Sitemap with Wireframes

Then I connected the individual wireframes in an overall sitemap. This approach allowed me to visualize the app’s architecture and make sure everything was clear, logical and consistent.

Next Step

To create an interactive prototype and conduct a user test to validate my assumption about the design.
